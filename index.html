🎓 Teacher Notes

  • Show this tab first — give students 30 sec to read before switching
  • Ask: "Which rule breaks a product? A fraction? An exponent?"
  • Predict before practice: "Which rule will we use most often?"
Product Rule
logb(M · N) = logb(M) + logb(N)
Log of a product → sum of logs
Quotient Rule
logb(M / N) = logb(M) − logb(N)
Log of a quotient → difference of logs
Power Rule
logb(Mp) = p · logb(M)
Exponent becomes a coefficient
Remember
logb(b) = 1     logb(1) = 0     logb(bx) = x
These three come up constantly when simplifying
